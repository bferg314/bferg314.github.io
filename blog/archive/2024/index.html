    Is It Still Mine? Wrestling with AI and the Joy of Creation

    I do quite a bit of chatting with ChatGPT and Claude. They are both sounding boards for philosophical and creative ideas that would bore most of the people I am surrounded with. Certainly not the fault of those lucky enough to interact with me every day, but I’m sure I already sound like a broken record on some topics, and other topics are just not what they are interested in. Funny how your interests and tastes can go down paths that diverge so much from your people.

    It’s true that No Man is an Island, but at the same time, all humans are truly islands. We are shaped by the currents that surround us—our connections, our tools, and the ideas that drift into our lives. No one creates in isolation, even if it sometimes feels that way. AI has become one of those currents for me, bridging the gaps between my isolated interests and offering a collaborative partner where none existed before.

    That being said, I’ve been wrestling with the idea of creating alongside AI. It feels both liberating and disorienting—how much of what I produce is truly mine? But I’ve come to realize that the joy of creation doesn’t depend on where the spark comes from. It’s in the act itself: shaping ideas, refining thoughts, and sharing something new with the world. Of course, there are valid concerns about how this changes the creative landscape, particularly for those whose livelihoods depend on traditional forms of creation. But every leap in technology—from the printing press to digital media—has reshaped the creative world, often in ways we couldn’t predict. While it’s natural to resist change, perhaps the best response is to embrace it and let it inspire us to create more, not less.

    Here are some additional thoughts on the joy of creating.

    Imagine a world where technology and society are arranged to support the simple joy of creating for its own sake. A world where we could explore every rabbit hole that sparks our curiosity without guilt or hesitation, hum a tune and have it transformed into a song we could treasure for months, or turn a fleeting idea for a film into something our family could watch that evening. AI offers a glimpse of this possibility—tools that amplify our creativity rather than replace it. It’s not a perfect system, but it’s a step toward a future where creation becomes as natural and accessible as breathing.

    Ideally, all our creative endeavors would purely be for the sake of creating, if only society and technology could be arranged in a way to support that wonderful dream. Until then, we can lean into the tools we have, embrace the joy of the process, and share what we create, no matter where it comes from or how it takes shape.
